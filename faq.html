FREQUENTLY ASKED QUESTIONS

How can I apply?

We are no longer accepting applications.

Who is eligible to attend the Cybertrain Junior Investigators 2.0 camp?

The GenCyber Junior Investigators 2.0 is open to middle school students.

What is the goal of Cybertrain Junior Investigators 2.0 Camp?

The Junior Investigators 2.0 summer experience offers 40 student participants from minority, women, and disadvantaged backgrounds in 6-8th grade the opportunity to learn and explore the cybersecurity first principles, as set forth by the Cybertrain program, in a hands-on learner centered environment led by Georgia Southern University faculty. The summer experience, based in the Cybertrain goals and cybersecurity first principles, provides a technology and pedagogical centric approach augmented by learning experiences outside of the classroom via custom designed activities and endeavors designed to reinforce the goals and principles of Cybertrain.

When is the Cybertrain Junior Investigators 2.0 camp?

The Cybertrain Junior Investigators 2.0 will be held June 3rd-8th. The Cybertrain Junior Investigators 2.0 are FREE to all accepted students.

What is the Cybertrain program?

Cybertrain's mission is to grow the number of students studying cybersecurity in the United States. The program includes summer camps across the nation designed for elementary, middle, and high school students, and teachers. The camps focus on engaging the learners with sound cybersecurity principles and teaching techniques. The Cybertrain program operates on 3 primary goals: Increase interest in cybersecurity careers and diversity in the cybersecurity workforce of the Nation. Help all students understand correct and safe on-line behavior. Improve teaching methods for delivering cybersecurity content for K-12 programs.

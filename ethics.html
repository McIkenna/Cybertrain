Protecting Yourself from Scams

Report any scams you find

  • o If you recognize a scam, report it so that no one else falls victim
  • o Know how to identify scams. Scams often contain the following attributes
  • o Alarmist messages and threats of account closures
  • o Promises of money for little or no effort
  • o Deals that sound too good to be true
  • o Requests to donate to a charitable organization after a disaster that has been in the news.
  • o Bad grammar and misspelling

    • What to do if you think you’ve been the victim of a scam?

  • o Change the passwords or PINs on all your online accounts that you think might be compromised.
  • o Place a fraud alert on your credit reports. Check with your bank or financial advisor if you're not sure how to do this.
  • o Contact the bank or the online merchant directly. Do not follow the link in the fraudulent email message.
  • o If you know of any accounts that were accessed or opened fraudulently, close those accounts.
  • o Routinely review your bank and credit card statements monthly for unexplained charges or inquiries that you didn't initiate.

    • Recognizing Phishing Email Messages, Links, or Phone Calls

    Phishing email messages, websites, and phone calls are designed to steal money. Cybercriminals can do this by installing malicious software on your computer or stealing personal information off your computer. Here are some steps on how to recognize and avoid phishing attempts.
  • o Common things to check for in an email
  • o Bad spelling and grammar, suspicious links
  • o Threats
  • o Spoofing popular websites or companies
  • o Common phishing phone call techniques
  • o Saying they have a way to “fix your computer”
  • o Offering free software
    • If you identify a phone call phisher, report them using the FTC Complaint Assistant Form. If you identify a phisher through email or a website, you can use Microsoft Tools to report the website or the email.

    DO:

  • 1) Use the Internet to communicate and interact with other people. You can use email and social media to effectively communicate with your friends, family, or even with other students on a project.
  • 2) Report cyberbullying.Cyberbullying is the use of electronic communication to bully a person, typically by sending messages of an intimidating or threatening nature.
  • 3) use the Internet for research and information.
  • 4) Enjoy music, videos and games on the Internet
  • 5) Shop, bank and pay bills online.
  • 6) Use the Internet to expand your social and business networks

    • DON'T:

  • 1) Cyberbully others
  • 2) Encourage cyberbullies
  • 3) Use copyrighted information as your own. Copyright is the legal right, given to someone to print, publish, perform, film, or record literary, artistic, or musical material, and to authorize others to do the same.
  • 4) Download or share copyrighted information
  • 5) Share personal information too easily

    • By following these simple Dos and Don'ts, you will have amazing cyberethics!

    Taking Charge of Your Online Reputation

    Evaluate your online reputation

  • o Use search engines to find anything that might be viewed negatively regarding you.
  • o Search engines are websites like Google, Yahoo, or Bing.
  • o Protect your online reputation
  • o Think before you share
  • o Treat others as you would like to be treated
  • o Stay aware of what the Internet is saying about you
  • o Polish your professional reputation
  • o Publish positive information about yourself
  • o Consider separating professional and personal profiles.
  • o Restore your online reputation
  • o If you find anything negative about you, act quickly
  • o Kindly ask whoever posted the negative comments to take down their comments

    • Protecting Your Privacy on the Internet

  • o Think before you share any personal information
  • o Do not post anything online that you would not want made public.
  • o Minimize details that identify you or your whereabouts.
  • o Keep your account numbers, user names, and passwords secret.

    • Monitor what other users post

  • o Search for your name on the Internet using at least two search engines. Search for text and images. If you find sensitive information on a website about yourself, look for contact information on the website and send a request to have your information removed.
  • o Regularly review what others write about you on blogs and social networking websites. Ask friends not to post photos of you or your family without your permission. If you feel uncomfortable with material such as information or photos that are posted on others' websites, ask for it to be removed.

    • Guard your information

  • o Use an internet firewall
    • A firewall is a part of a computer system or network that is designed to block unauthorized access while permitting outward communication.
  • o Create strong passphrases
  • o Use an antivirus software
    • Antivirus software is software designed to detect and destroy computer viruses.
  • o Don’t share your passphrase with anyone
  • o Don’t use the same password for everything. If you do this, someone only needs to find the password of one of your accounts to know them all
  • o Do all sensitive or private business on your home computer
  • o Don’t pay bills or enter your credit card information on public computers

    • Protect yourself from fraud

  • o Think before you click to visit a website or call a number in a suspicious email or phone message—both could be phony.
  • o Use a phishing filter
